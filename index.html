Edible Mushrooms

White button mushrooms

When raw button mushrooms are a white or very light brown. When cooked button mushrooms turn a lovely golden brown. These mushrooms have a mild flavour. That becomes stronger when cooked. You can eat button mushrooms raw, fried with spices or added to a tasty mix with chicken.

Portobello Mushrooms

The portobello mushroom can have a large dark brown or tan cap, underneath the cap it is a dark brown colour. These mushrooms have a smoky, earthy flavour when fried or grilled. The scientific name for the portobello mushroom is agaricus bisporus.

Shiitake Mushrooms

Shiitake mushrooms have a brown umbrella like cap and a white stem. The stem of the shiitake mushroom can be quite fibrous to eat however they give a nice rich flavour to broths and stews.

Poisonous Mushrooms

Death cap

The colour of a death cap can vary. The caps are off white and may have tints of green or yellow. The older mushroom caps become almost brown. Scientific name Amanita phalloides. It is deadly poisonous, with symptoms only showing 8-24 hours after eating it. It is deadly if ingested, beginning with vomiting, cramps and diarrhea after 6 - 24 hours of eating it. After 3-5 days the person can experience liver, kidney or organ failure.

Destroying angel

The cap can be pure white or have a yellow, pinkish or tan center. Scientific name Amanita virosa. It is deadly poisonous, with symptoms only showing 8-24 hours after eating it. It will start with vomiting, diarrhea and cramps, later kidney or liver dysfunction which can lead to death. There is no known antidote.

Fly agaric

The fly agaric is usually a red mushroom with white spots. This mushroom is poisonous when raw and is infamously known for psychoactive and hallucinogenic properties. When prepared properly you can eat it, however it can cause nausea and inebriation if it is not prepared correctly.
